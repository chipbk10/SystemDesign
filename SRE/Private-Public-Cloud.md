A private cloud does not have all the characteristics of a public cloud. While they share the same underlying technology, they differ fundamentally in ownership, access, and scalability.

## Core Differences

* **Shared Resources**: Public clouds use multi-tenant environments shared among many organizations. Private clouds are single-tenant infrastructure dedicated entirely to one organization.
* **Scalability**: Public clouds offer near-infinite, instant scalability on demand. Private clouds are limited by the physical hardware owned or leased by the organization.
* **Capital Cost**: Public clouds require no upfront hardware investment, operating on a pay-as-you-go model. Private clouds require high initial capital expenditure for hardware and setup.
* **Maintenance**: Public cloud providers handle all hardware maintenance, updates, and physical security. Private cloud maintenance is the sole responsibility of the internal IT team.

## Shared Characteristics

* **Self-Service**: Both allow users to provision resources through an automated portal.
* **Virtualization**: Both use software to divide physical hardware into virtual machines.
* **Resource Pooling**: Both dynamically allocate computing power, storage, and network bandwidth.
